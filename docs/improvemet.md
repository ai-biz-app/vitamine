## Areas to Improve (Actionable)

### 1. The "Start Here" CTA Goes Nowhere
At the top, you have a **"Start Here"** button, but the page appears to be a single landing page. If the plan is a multi-page site (vitamin detail pages, a quiz, etc.), this button should route to the first meaningful step. If it is meant to scroll to the "At a Glance" section, make it an anchor link. Right now it feels like a dead end.

### 2. The Quiz CTA at the Bottom Is Vague
"Get a personalized breakdown in under 2 minutes" — if this links to an actual quiz or tool, great. If it is just a placeholder, remove it until the feature is live. A broken promise hurts trust more than no promise at all.

### 3. Missing Individual Vitamin Pages
From the homepage, there is no path to learn about **Vitamin A**, **B12**, **D**, etc. If you have these pages elsewhere, they need to be linked here. If you don't have them yet, this is the highest-value content to add next. People search for specific vitamins far more than they search for "vitamin guide."

### 4. No Evidence or Citations
You make strong claims (e.g., "1B+ people deficient in vitamin D," "The word vitamin was coined in 1912"). These build credibility, but without a source link or footnote, skeptical readers will question them. Add light citations — even just a small superscript link to NIH, WHO, or a peer-reviewed source.

### 5. SEO & Discoverability
- **Page title**: "ClearlyVitamins - Essential Vitamin Guide" is fine, but consider adding a benefit-driven meta description.
- **No visible blog or search**: Long-term, search traffic will come from specific queries like "foods high in vitamin D" or "vitamin B12 deficiency symptoms." You need content that matches those queries.
- **Structured data**: Add `FAQPage` or `HowTo` schema for the "6 Things" section to improve search snippet appearance.

### 6. Navigation & Wayfinding
If this is part of a larger site (with language switching, detail pages, etc.), the global navigation is not visible in the hero. Users should always know where they are and where they can go.

### 7. Visual Hierarchy
The content is clean, but it is very text-heavy. Consider:
- Adding a simple comparison table for Water-Soluble vs. Fat-Soluble vitamins.
- Using icons or small illustrations for the "6 Things" instead of just numbers.
- Breaking up the "Key Insights" section with pull quotes or bolded one-liners.

### 8. Accessibility
- Ensure the green color used for "ClearlyVitamins" has sufficient contrast against white.
- Check that the emoji icons (💧, 🥑) have `aria-label` or are hidden from screen readers if they are decorative.

---

## Quick Wins (Do These First)

1. **Fix the "Start Here" button** — make it scroll to the first content section or remove it.
2. **Add a footer** with links to About, Privacy, Contact, and language switcher if you serve Korean users.
3. **Link out to vitamin detail pages** from the "13 Essential vitamins" stat — even if they are stub pages, it signals depth.
4. **Add one citation** to the most surprising fact (e.g., the 1912 coining of "vitamin") to establish a pattern of trustworthiness.

---

## Bottom Line

The site has a **solid foundation**: clear voice, good structure, and trustworthy tone. The main gap is between the *promise* of depth ("personalized breakdown," "essential guide") and the *reality* of a single landing page. The next phase should focus on **expanding into specific, searchable vitamin content** and ensuring every CTA has a real destination.
